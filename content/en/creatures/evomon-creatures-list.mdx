### Creature Rarity, Stats, and How to Get the Best Ones

Not all EvoMon are created equal. The game uses a sophisticated rarity and stat system that determines a creature's potential. Understanding this is crucial for building a competitive team. The system revolves around **Talents**, **Natures**, and **Ranks**.

#### Understanding Ranks and Talents

Each creature has a rank (e.g., A, S, Triple-S) that affects its stat bonuses. The higher the rank, the more bonus points are distributed to its base stats.

| Rank | Stat Bonus per Point | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A | +8 | A decent creature for early to mid-game. |
| S | +12 | A strong creature with good potential. |
| Triple-S (SSS) | +15 | The rarest and most powerful rank. |

Player experience confirms that you can reroll these talent bonuses to focus on a specific stat, like Defense or Attack. This allows for deep customization of your creatures.

#### Natures and Move Types

Natures provide a 10% boost to one stat and a 10% reduction to another. For example, an "Attack +10%, Special Defense -10%" nature is ideal for a physical attacker. The game also distinguishes between **Physical** and **Special** moves. Physical moves are close-range attacks (like "Dash Punch"), while Special moves are ranged (like "Cinder"). Always match your creature's nature to its primary move type.
