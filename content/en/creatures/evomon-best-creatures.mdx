The reasoning is simple: the first major area, Verdant Valley, is filled with rock and grass-type enemies. The final boss of this zone is also a rock type. ## How to Maximize Your Creature's Potential

Catching a great creature is only half the battle. To make your **Evomon best creatures** truly unstoppable, you need to understand the game’s core progression systems: Talents, Natures, and Ascension.

### Understanding Talents and Ranks

Every creature has a rank (A, S, or SS) that determines how many bonus stat points it gets. An S-rank creature gets a +12 to a random stat, while an SS-rank gets +15. You can reroll these bonuses using in-game currency to ensure your creature’s strengths are maximized.

| Rank | Bonus Stat Points | How to Obtain |
|------|-------------------|---------------|
| A | +8 | Common from wild catches |
| S | +12 | Rare from wild catches or eggs |
| SS | +15 | Extremely rare; guaranteed with special balls (e.g., Prismatic Ball) |

### Mastering Natures

Natures are a simple buff/debuff system. You can reroll them to get a +10% boost to one stat and a -10% penalty to another. For a physical attacker, you want a Nature that boosts Attack and lowers a stat you don’t need, like Special Defense.

### The Ascension Quest: Unlocking True Power

You will notice your creatures stop leveling up at level 30. To progress further, you must complete the **Ascension Quest**. This is a series of tasks given by quest givers like Lillian in each major zone. Completing these quests raises your Ascension level, allowing your creatures to level up to 60 and beyond. Player experience shows that focusing on these quests is the fastest way to progress.
